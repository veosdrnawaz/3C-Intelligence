Methodology & Mathematical Pipeline

Rigorous processing of raw municipal indicators using standardized unsupervised learning frameworks.

01

1. StandardScaler Normalization

To prevent scale discrepancy—where large-magnitude features like Population dominate distance calculations—we perform standard Z-score normalization on all variables, yielding a mean of zero and standard deviation of one.

02

2. Cluster Hyperparameter Selection

Using data-driven Elbow curves (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares) and Silhouette Coefficients across $K \in [2, 10]$, K=3 represents the optimal local maximum for municipal risk partitioning and inter-cluster separation.

03

3. Empirically Weighted Crime Index Equation

Qualitative risk classification is determined by ordering the cluster centroids based on a composite, severity-weighted Crime Score. This mathematical formulation prioritizes violent crime vectors over property crime indicators:

$$\text{Urban Risk Score} = \frac{\text{Murder Rate} \times 3 + \text{Assault Rate} \times 2 + \text{Theft Rate} \times 1}{6}$$
